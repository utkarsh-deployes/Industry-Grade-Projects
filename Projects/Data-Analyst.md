This page documents industry-grade, hireable projects for Data Analysts that reflect how analytics work is actually performed in companies.

---

## What Recruiters Expect From a Data Analyst

When recruiters evaluate data analyst projects, they look for evidence of:

- Ability to translate business problems into analytical questions
- Strong data cleaning and transformation skills
- Understanding of metrics, KPIs, and trade-offs
- SQL proficiency and structured querying
- Clear insights, not just charts
- Ability to communicate findings to non-technical stakeholders

Tools matter, but **thinking and impact matter more**.

---

## Common Resume Mistakes (What Not to Do)

Avoid data analyst projects such as:
- Random dashboards with no business context
- Kaggle notebooks with no decision-making angle
- Visualizations without insights or recommendations
- Synthetic datasets with unrealistic assumptions
- Projects that do not answer a clear business question

These projects do not demonstrate real analytics maturity.

## Hireable Industry-Grade Project Ideas

Each project below mirrors analytics work performed by data teams in startups and enterprises.

---

### Project 1: Customer Churn Analysis and Retention Strategy  
**Difficulty Level:** 6.5 / 10

#### Business Problem
A subscription-based business wants to understand why customers churn and how to reduce churn.

#### Why This Exists in Real Companies
Churn directly impacts revenue. Almost every product company invests heavily in churn analysis.

#### Core Analysis Tasks
- Define churn and retention metrics
- Clean and join customer, usage, and billing data
- Identify churn patterns by cohort, usage, and tenure
- Segment customers by risk level

#### Tools and Skills
- SQL for data extraction
- Python for analysis
- Visualization for trend analysis

#### Business Deliverables
- Churn drivers and risk segments
- Actionable retention recommendations
- Metrics to track improvement

#### Resume-Ready Bullet Points
- Analyzed customer churn patterns to identify high-risk segments
- Built cohort-based retention analysis to uncover churn drivers
- Delivered data-backed recommendations to improve retention

### Project 2: Revenue Funnel and Conversion Analysis  
**Difficulty Level:** 7 / 10

#### Business Problem
A product team wants to understand where users drop off in the acquisition and purchase funnel.

#### Why This Exists in Real Companies
Funnel optimization is a core analytics responsibility in growth-focused teams.

#### Core Analysis Tasks
- Define funnel stages and success metrics
- Analyze conversion rates between stages
- Identify bottlenecks and drop-off points
- Segment funnel performance by channel or user type

#### Tools and Skills
- SQL for funnel queries
- Python for deeper analysis
- Visualization for stakeholder reporting

#### Business Deliverables
- Funnel conversion breakdown
- Key drop-off insights
- Prioritized improvement areas

#### Resume-Ready Bullet Points
- Analyzed user funnels to identify conversion bottlenecks
- Built stage-level conversion metrics using SQL
- Provided insights to improve acquisition and revenue flow

### Project 3: Product Feature Impact Analysis  
**Difficulty Level:** 7.5 / 10

#### Business Problem
A company wants to understand whether a newly launched feature actually improved user engagement.

#### Why This Exists in Real Companies
Feature impact analysis guides product investment decisions.

#### Core Analysis Tasks
- Define success metrics before and after launch
- Perform cohort or A/B-style comparisons
- Control for confounding factors
- Quantify impact on engagement or retention

#### Tools and Skills
- SQL for event data analysis
- Python for statistical comparison
- Data visualization for clarity

#### Business Deliverables
- Feature impact assessment
- Quantified metric changes
- Clear recommendation on rollout or iteration

#### Resume-Ready Bullet Points
- Measured feature impact on user engagement using cohort analysis
- Designed pre- and post-launch performance comparisons
- Delivered insights to inform product roadmap decisions

## How to Choose the Right Project

- Choose projects with clear business questions
- Focus on decision-making, not visualization volume
- Document assumptions and limitations
- Be able to explain why insights matter to the business

Impact matters more than tool count.

---

## Interview Preparation Tip

Be prepared to explain:
- How metrics were defined
- Why certain analyses were chosen
- What decisions the insights informed
- Limitations and alternative interpretations

---

## Final Note

Strong data analysts are valued for clarity and judgment,  
not just technical execution.

Ask better questions.
